Do's & Dont's

Use a Mix of Characters

Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

Why: A mix of characters makes your password harder to guess and more secure.

Example: P@ssw0rd!23

Avoid Common Words and Patterns

Avoid using common words, phrases, or predictable patterns.

Why: Common words and patterns are easier for attackers to guess or crack.

Example: Instead of password123, use something like G7h!kL9@

Make It Long

Use a password that is at least 9 characters long.

Why: Longer passwords are more secure and harder to crack and are less targets of scams.

Example: Th1s!sAStr0ngP@ssw0rd

Use Unpredictable Combinations

Combine random words, numbers, and symbols that do not form a predictable pattern.

Why: Unpredictable combinations make it harder for attackers to guess your password.

Avoid Personal Information

Do not use easily accessible personal information such as your name, birthdate, or address.

Why: Personal information is often easy to guess or find out, making your password less secure.

Change Passwords Regularly

Update your passwords periodically, especially for important accounts.

Why: Regularly changing passwords reduces the risk of long-term exposure if a password is compromised.

Avoid Reusing Passwords

Use a unique password for each account.

Why: Reusing passwords increases the risk of multiple accounts being compromised if one password is exposed.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Whenever possible, enable 2FA for an extra layer of security.

Why: 2FA adds an additional step to the login process, making it more difficult for unauthorized users to access your accounts.
